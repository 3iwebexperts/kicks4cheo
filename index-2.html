Leadercast Live

Leadercast Live is the world's largest one-day leadership event, with more than 100,000 attendees participating at 700-plus locations in 20 countries around the globe. It is designed for those seeking inspiration and improvement in leadership, whether you attend as an individual or with a group.

WHY Attend Leadercast Live?

WHY Attend Leadercast Live?

Though Leadercast serves a community of leaders year-round with a wide array of leadership solutions ranging from executive coaching to online education, there’s nothing like being in the audience surrounded by thousands of fellow enthusiastic leaders at Leadercast Live. Take the opportunity to remove yourself from your normal business environment; it will free you up from everyday distractions to network, learn and be inspired.

The change of scenery often ignites creativity and helps leaders look at their businesses and lives in a different light. We believe spending time with attendees, vendors and experts, plus a staff whose only focus is to ensure you have a deeper level of engagement, creates a life-changing experience.

WHERE Does Leadercast Live Take Place?

Leadercast Live is proud to call metro Atlanta home and here in Brockville, Ontario Canada we are thrilled to be host site for 9 years running. Leadercast Brockville takes place at Centennial Road Church.

Craig Springer

Craig Springer is the Executive Director of Alpha USA; a program that mobilizes 50,000 volunteers and 350,000 participants annually in the US and over 1.5 million globally. Alpha is a simple idea of a great meal, a short talk and a meaningful discussion about life and faith. The key to Alpha is listening – it’s a judgement-free space where any point of view is respected, no one gets corrected and people can explore their thoughts together. Alpha USA is helping to transform the culture of churches in America and across the globe to listen more than we speak. Craig has been a leader and pastor in Chicago and Denver, and is a passionate hack at every mountain sport you can name.

Gayle King

Gayle King is a media titan, carving her way through the cutthroat media industry for more than 40 years. She is co-host of CBS This Morning and editor-at-large of the award-winning O, the Oprah Magazine. Gayle previously hosted The Gayle King Show, a live, weekday television interview program on OWN: The Oprah Winfrey Network. Before moving into print and radio, she worked for almost 25 years as a television news anchor.

Carla Harris

Carla Harris is vice chairman, managing director and senior advisor at Morgan Stanley, where she is responsible for increasing client connectivity and penetration to enhance revenue generation across the firm. In August 2013, she was appointed by President Barack Obama to chair the National Women’s Business Council. Carla has been named to Fortune’s list of “The 50 Most Powerful Black Executives in Corporate America.”

Marcus Samuelsson

Crowned champion of television shows Top Chef Masters and Chopped All Stars, and serving as a mentor on ABC’s The Taste.

He most recently announced the opening of his newest restaurant, Marcus, coming to Montreal in Spring 2019

Marcus Samuelsson is the acclaimed chef behind many restaurants worldwide including Red Rooster Harlem, Red Rooster Shoreditch, and Marcus B&P. Samuelsson was the youngest person to ever receive a three-star review from The New York Times and has won multiple James Beard Foundation Awards including Best Chef of New York City.

Carey Lohrenz

The first female F-14 Tomcat fighter pilot in the U.S. Navy, Carey has flown missions worldwide, operating in fast-moving, dynamic environments where consistency and precision are needed. Carey now shares the lessons she learned in the all-male environment of fighter pilots with others—lessons like building credibility, how to lead high-performance organizations and diversity training.

Ian Morgan Cron

Ian is a best-selling author, psychotherapist, Enneagram teacher, Episcopal priest and host of the Typology podcast. Ian uses the Enneagram personality types to help leaders grow with self-awareness and emotional wisdom. His latest book, The Road Back to You: An Enneagram Journey to Self-Discovery, has brought the Enneagram into corporations and organizations as a unique tool for developing leaders. He is a key advisor to clients such as Discovery Channel, Ramsey Solutions and the Michael Hyatt Company, among others.

Jen Bricker

Born without legs, Jen defied all odds when she began her career as an acrobat and aerialist. When she was born, her biological parents left her at the hospital, but she was adopted by a family who encouraged her that anything is possible and to not let ‘can’t’ be part of her vocabulary. This mentality led her to follow her childhood passion for gymnastics, competing against able-bodied athletes to become a state champion in power tumbling. Since then, Jen toured around the globe in Britney Spears’ World Tour and is now an accomplished speaker and author of Everything Is Possible.

Jim Loehr, Ed.D.

Jim is a groundbreaking performance psychologist and author whose science-based energy management training system is recognized worldwide. With more than 30 years of experience in applied research, he believes the single-most important factor in success is the strength of one’s character, which he says can be trained in a similar manner to muscle strength.

Michael Hyatt

Founder and CEO of Michael Hyatt & Company, Michael is a leadership author, speaker and coach. His company specializes in transformative life events, workshops and planning tools. Before starting his own leadership development firm, Michael became a best-selling author with his books Living Forward and Platform, among others. His latest book, Your Best Year Ever, released in January 2018. He and his wife of almost 40 years, Gail, live outside Nashville.

Dr. Mae Jemison

Not only is Mae an engineer, physician and NASA astronaut, she is the first woman of color from any country in the world to go into space; she flew aboard the Space Shuttle Endeavor in 1992. After six years as an astronaut, she became a strong voice for science literacy across the globe. Mae has founded The Earth We Share international science camp, technology consulting firm The Jemison Group and medical technology company BioSentient Corporation.

Mae was inducted into the National Women’s Hall of Fame in 1993 and the International Space Hall of Fame in 2004. She is currently a member of the National Academy of Sciences’ Institute of Medicine, a trustee of Morehouse College, and a speaker on health care, social responsibility, technology and motivation.
